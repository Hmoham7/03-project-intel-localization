1968

Intel Founded

The company is formed, setting a long-term path for innovation.

Learn more

Robert Noyce and Gordon Moore rename NM Electronics to Intel Corporation, laying the foundation for a global technology leader.

1971

First Microprocessor

A breakthrough that reshapes computing and efficiency.

Learn more

Intel debuts the 4004, the world's first commercial microprocessor, sparking a wave of smaller, more powerful devices.

1978

8086 Processor

A durable architecture that powers modern computing.

Learn more

The 8086 processor establishes the x86 architecture that still powers many PCs and servers today.

1985

386 Processor

Performance gains enable more work with less energy.

Learn more

Intel introduces the 32-bit 386 processor, boosting performance and enabling more efficient multitasking.

2006

Peak GHG Emissions

A turning point that drives long-term reduction strategies.

Learn more

Intel reaches peak operational emissions and begins major investments in abatement, renewables, and efficient manufacturing.

2020

RISE Strategy

Clear goals for climate, water, and waste.

Learn more

Intel launches its RISE strategy and 2030 goals to accelerate progress on climate action, water stewardship, and circularity.

2022

Net-Zero By 2040

A long-term pledge to transform operations.

Learn more

Intel commits to net-zero greenhouse gas emissions (Scope 1 and 2) across global operations by 2040.

2023

Renewable Electricity

Near total renewable energy usage worldwide.

Learn more

Intel reaches 99% renewable electricity usage globally, cutting operational emissions and advancing its climate targets.

2024

Sustainability Summit

Collaboration across the semiconductor ecosystem.

Learn more

Intel hosts its first Sustainability Summit to align suppliers, policymakers, and leaders on next-gen responsible manufacturing.
